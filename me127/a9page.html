Design Review

Notes:

Went back to claw design.

I raised the mounting point of the claw to make it fit in the 12 in box.

I wanted to decrease the length between the front contact point and the rear contact point so I shortened it while trying to keep it somewhat stable.

Shortening this length helps with climbing a slope from the formula we derived in our notes during class.

I placed the battery pack as low as possible under the body because lower center of gravity also helps with climbing.

The robot arm was inspired by the arm I saw on Tim's robot but we went different ways for the mechanism that picks up the ball.

I liked the claw I used because it was able to pick up the ball even in corners.

I forced the caster wheel to stop rotating as Sreeram suggested this.

The caster wheel seems to have a mind of its own. When turning, it will make steering very difficult as there was no restriction on its movement.

The downside with completly restricting its movement meant the rear servos would just have to drag it across the ground, being a source of friction.

I tried a scissor lift and well that had many issues.

There was too much friction.

The servo was not strong enough.

Benefits with this over the claw were that it could extend much higher, eliminating the need to climb the ramp.
